# Single-Cell Multiomic Analysis of LAM Reveals a HOX–PBX Regulatory Network

**Publication:** Science Advances, 2023  
**Role:** Co–First Author  
**Domain:** Single-cell genomics, regulatory network inference, chromatin accessibility  
**Data Types:** scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq  
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adf8549

---

## Project Overview

This project used **single-cell multiomic sequencing** to identify gene regulatory mechanisms that drive the survival of **lymphangioleiomyomatosis (LAM)** cells, a rare and poorly understood disease. By integrating transcriptional and chromatin accessibility data, we uncovered a **HOX–PBX gene regulatory network** with a central role in disease cell survival.

From an industry perspective, this work demonstrates how large-scale, noisy biological data can be transformed into **interpretable regulatory insights** using computational pipelines and network-based analysis.

---

## Problem Statement

Single-cell sequencing generates massive, high-dimensional datasets, but translating these data into **actionable biological mechanisms** remains challenging. Key questions included:

- How can regulatory drivers be identified from heterogeneous cell populations?
- How can chromatin accessibility (ATAC-seq) be integrated with expression data to infer causality?
- Which gene networks distinguish disease-driving cells from surrounding tissue?

---

## My Contributions (Co–First Author)

I led the **computational network generation and ATAC-seq analysis**, with responsibilities including:

- Designing and implementing **gene regulatory network inference pipelines**
- Performing **scATAC-seq preprocessing, peak calling, and accessibility analysis**
- Integrating **scRNA-seq and scATAC-seq** to link transcription factor activity with downstream gene expression
- Identifying and validating **HOX–PBX network structure** as a key regulator of LAM cell survival
- Contributing to data interpretation, figure generation, and manuscript writing

This role required both **statistical rigor** and **engineering discipline** to ensure scalability and reproducibility.
