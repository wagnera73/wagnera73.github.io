# Hello I am Andrew Wagner

Hello and welcome to my portfolio. I am a graduate student in the University of Cincinnati's Computer Science Department. My research focus is developing and applying state of the art of machine learning algorithms to multimodal problems. Previous projects include using optimal transport and graph attention networks (GATs) to improve entity alignment between multimodal knowledge graphs. Current work includes model agnostic meta learning and few-short learning.

I also have extensive experience as a Data Analyst at Cincinnati Children's Hospital Medical Center. I have several publications that utilize high-throughput next-generation sequencing technology (bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, single-nucleus ATAC-seq, and 10X Multiome) to study transcriptomic and epigenetic changes in lung disease. In addition, I have several publications integrating multimodal data to predict transcription regulatory networks and identify candidate genes driving disease phenotype.

Finally, I also have extensive experience as a web developer and data engineer. My time at UC's Simulation Center saw me developing a data pipeline in conjunction with Procter & Gamble. This pipeline performed noise-correction and event detection on data from IoT-connected devices. After fine-tuning the pipeline, I developed a FastAPI - based UI for ease of use. I was also charged with coordinating with team leads to establish requirements and features of the algorithm. In total, I assisted five teams across North America, Europe, and Japan in applying my pipeline.
